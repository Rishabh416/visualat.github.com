Writing

Students with visual impairments and blindness confront several obstacles while completing writing assignments that require traditional writing and visual writing, such as ink on paper or typing on a computer screen. People with visual impairment and blindness may struggle to master the mechanics of writing (e.g., punctuation and spelling), as well as taking notes that rely on visually oriented concepts and information. Although this visual information is not widely available in a format that visually impaired students can easily access, there are some devices that can be used for science teaching, such as tactile maps, tactile diagram set for sciences, tactile anatomy atlas, animal models, plants, or three-dimensional models objects, such as the DNA twist model.
