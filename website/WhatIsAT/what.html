What is Visual Assistive Technology

AT for those who are visually impaired (limited vision) or blind is based on the use of other senses, such as touch or vibration, sound, and smell. For example, Braille and embossed print, need touch to provide information for visually impaired and blind individuals. AT development, including technology for those with vision impairment, has accelerated in recent years, allowing new technologies to arise that help those who are visually impaired complete tasks that they otherwise could not. A couple examples of assistive technology that help the visually impared include:

  • Glasses/lenses
  • Braille
  • Optical Magnifier
  • Color filter
  • Walking sticks
